Car Ownership Trends in Australian

The automobile industry has seen significant evolution over the years. In Australia, cars aren't just a mode of transportation; they are an integral part of the household. Understanding automobile ownership trends can provide insights into the socio-economic dynamics, environmental impact, and preferences of Australian citizens. This webpage delves deep into the patterns, variations, and implications of car ownership across different Australian households.

The three areas that unfold in the visualization below are Car ownership distribution, the economic status of the population, and environmental factors.

In the Visualization, five nationally representative countries were selected to explore the relationship between public health and life expectancy.

Among them, it can be seen that the best improvement in public health is in China, which has risen from 13% to 68% over time, and the most obvious change that follows is the concomitant increase in life expectancy.

In other countries, public health has risen less, but it can also be seen that life expectancy has steadily increased as public health has grown. Therefore, it can be seen that the state of public health is one of the factors affecting life expectancy.
